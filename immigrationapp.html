    UX Design and Research - Immigrants Resources Mobile Application

Problem

Countless immigrants who come to the United States, often hoping for opportunities to improve their quality of living, do not speak English and therefore struggle to settle down for a lengthy period of time. From my personal experience and my time spent conversing with Chinese-American parents, I have learned that it is overwhelmingly difficult for immigrants to take full advantage of the resources available even if they have friends or family around. Some entire immigrant families are still unaware of many useful resources such as Medicaid and emergency food pantries because of the English language barrier.

My Goal

My goal is to design a free mobile application that aggregates local resources (e.g. grocery stores, churches, community centers), including directions (using Google Maps APIs) and contact information, for immigrants.

Key Features

- Personalized results based on personal information. For example, college students will see more resources related to school (Can be turned on or off).

- Notifications for local events that can be extremely beneficial to immigrants, which include food pantries, assistance with applying for healthcare, etc.

- Option to select from a variety of languages that translates the app as well as the names of the resources and events using Google Translation APIs.

Research Process

In order to fully understand the audience that I am designing this app for, I interviewed 10 immigrants from a variety of socioeconomic backgrounds and ethnicities. The interviews are semi-structured and lasted for about 20 to 40 minutes.

I asked questions in three categories:

Experience with Digital Technology

Interactions with Community and Individuals

Goals (Short-Term and Long-Term)

Personas

After these interviews, I analyzed my interview notes, discovered patterns between my interviewees, and built two user personas based on my discoveries.

My next objective is to create some wireframes and start to brainstorm the actual design of the app with the information I have. Stay tuned.
