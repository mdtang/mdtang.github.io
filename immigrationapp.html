Problem

Countless immigrants who come to the United States, often hoping for opportunities to improve their quality of living, do not speak English and therefore struggle to settle down for a lengthy period of time. From my personal experiences and my time spent conversing with many Chinese-American parents, I have learned of the overwhelming difficulties immigrants face upon due to the language barrier. Many cannot take advantage of the useful resources available (Medicaid, food pantries, English tutoring) because of the language barrier.

My Goal

I seek to design a free mobile application that aggregates local resources (e.g. grocery stores, churches, community centers), including directions (using Google Maps APIs) and contact information, for immigrants. In the near future, I hope to design a desktop version as well.

Key Features

  1. Personalized results based on country of origin and English language proficiency.
  2. Dedicated app section for local events such as food pantry, free health check-ups, etc.
  3. Directions to local resources with Google Maps
  4. Option to select from a variety of languages that translates the app as well as the names of the resources and events using Google Translation APIs.

Research Process

In order to fully understand the audience that I am designing this app for, I interviewed 10 immigrants from a variety of socioeconomic backgrounds and ethnicities. The interviews are semi-structured and lasted for about 20 to 40 minutes.

I asked questions in three categories:

Experience with Digital Technology
Interactions with Community and Individuals
Goals (Short-Term and Long-Term)

Personas

After these interviews, I analyzed my notes taken during the interviews, discovered patterns between my interviewees, and built two user personas based on my discoveries.
