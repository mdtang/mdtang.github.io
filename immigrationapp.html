Problem

Countless immigrants who come to the United States, often hoping for opportunities to improve their quality of living, do not speak English and therefore struggle to settle down for a lengthy period of time. From my personal experiences and my time spent conversing with many Chinese-American parents, I have learned of the overwhelming difficulties immigrants face upon due to the language barrier. Many cannot take advantage of the useful resources available (Medicaid, food pantries, English tutoring) because of the language barrier.

My Goal

I seek to design a free mobile application that aggregates local resources and services (e.g. grocery stores, churches, community centers), including directions (using Google Maps APIs) and contact information, for immigrants. In the near future, I hope to design a desktop version as well.

Key Features

  1. Personalized results based on country of origin and English language proficiency.
  2. Dedicated app section for local events such as food pantry, free health check-ups, etc.
  3. Directions to local resources/services with Google Maps
  4. Option to select from a variety of languages that translates the app as well as the names of the resources, services, and events using Google Translation APIs.

Research Process

In order to fully understand the audience that I am designing this app for, I interviewed 10 immigrants from a variety of socioeconomic backgrounds and ethnicities. The interviews are semi-structured and lasted for about 20 to 40 minutes.

I asked questions in three categories:

  1. Experience with Digital Technology
  2. Interactions with Community and Individuals
  3. Goals (Short-Term and Long-Term)

Personas

After these interviews, I analyzed my notes taken during the interviews, discovered patterns between my interviewees, and built two user personas based on my discoveries.

One important thing to take away from my research is that most of my interviewees feel uncomfortable looking for new resources or services in person, and would rather rely on their social circle for local information. I want to help them break this barrier.

Use Cases

Before designing the app, I wanted to outline some of the use cases to identify how exactly the app would be used.

Use Case 1:

Actor Newly-Arrived Single Immigrant Mother from Lebanon
Scenario Immigrant mother is looking for a free language tutoring service to learn English and a food pantry service to receive food assistance.
Triggering Event The mother is trying to save money for her son and herself. She needs to learn English to improve her chances of getting a stable job, and also save money in other ways.
Precondition She has access to a smartphone.

Flow of Events:

1 She searches for a language assistance organization on the app.
2 She finds the one the likes and looks for more details.
3 She reads the type of language services offered and notices the address and opening hours.
4 She looks for directions from her apartment on the app.
5 She calls the organization to inquire and set up a possible appointment.
6 She looks for and finds a nearby organization that offers weekly food pantry services.
7 She notes the type of food available and the days of the week food is given out.
7 She looks for directions from her apartment on the app and decides to visit.
Outcome She finds an organization that offers free language tutoring services and an organization that offers free food pantry services.

Use Case 2:

Actor Newly-Arrived Graduate Student from China
Scenario This student is looking to find the cheapest stores to purchase groceries and a Christian church organization that has people who speak her language.
Triggering Event The student has just moved to her new apartment and wants to plan for weekly trips to church and to shop for groceries.
Precondition She has access to a smartphone.

Flow of Events:

1 He searches for a grocery store on the app.
2 He finds the one the likes and looks for more details.
3 He reads the type of groceries offered and notices the address and opening hours.
4 He looks for directions from her apartment on the app.
5 He plans for a trip to the store for Saturday.
6 He looks for and finds a nearby Chinese Christian Church organization.
7 He notes dates for events, sermons, bible studies, and more.
7 He looks for directions from her apartment on the app and plans for a trip on Sunday.
Outcome He finds a grocery store nearby and a Chinese Christian church nearby.

*It is assumed here that the app will change its language based on the country the user chooses. Here it would be in Chinese.

When a new user starts the app for the first time, they are approached with two questions, which are:

  • Which country are you from?
  • How well do you speak and read English?

These two questions are asked because the app presents services that are the most relevant to the user on the top of the screen. The app would also change to different languages based on the user's responses.

On the main screen, the user chooses from different types of services, such as grocery stores, language assistance organizations, etc. I created two paths for the user to follow, one for groceries and the other for language assistance. I included the address, rating, and languages spoken of the service on the services select page before the user decides to tap on a specific service.

Then, the user is shown business hours and contact number of the service, a directions map, and more detailed offerings of the service called “what is offered?”. For the latter addition, users are able to view the special offerings that this service has available, which in the first path would be seafood, vegetables, etc., the other path would show the types of language teaching and translations.

In the near future, I hope to design a desktop version of this app because I am aware that many immigrants do not have access to smartphones.
