I grew up in an impoverished, constantly-moving home with mostly the support of a single mother for eleven years in the United States. We moved multiple times when I was little; I once lived in an 80 sq. ft. room with my mother, having to deal with the unsanitized living conditions while attempting to succeed in school. But more importantly, I knew to value the importance of education and self-expression. In middle school and high school, I participated in concert choir and musical theater and set up my own college prep workshop for prospective college students who desperately needed academic assistance. Eventually, I fulfilled the dream of going to the University of Michigan. Now I am working hard to build my skills, coursework, and network so I can eventually become an UX designer in the technology or video game industry.
