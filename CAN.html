Problem

In the southern neighborhood of Ann Arbor, an organization called the Community Action Network (CAN) has developed Bryant Community Center into a stable resource and haven for local residents and underprivileged families. Some of its initiatives include weekly food pantries with fresh meat and vegetables and after-school programs for elementary school kids. CAN needed a responsible staff member to oversee fellowship and religious meetings that are held at the center on a regular basis. For instance, former alcoholics and drug addicts need a private space to discuss their recovery journey together.

Solution

After learning about the service that CAN has committed to supporting for decades, I decided to join as the only weekend staff and administer the center for incoming groups and food clients. Even though I faced a hectic class workload, I knew that giving up a small amount of my time would be of invaluable assistance to countless families.
