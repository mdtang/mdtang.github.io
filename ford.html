    Education/Public Policy Research - Ford School of Public Policy

Problem

I wanted to understand the effectiveness of Michigan K-8 charter schools, specifically that of the National Heritage Academy (NHA) schools, and the impact (or lack thereof) of their educational policies based on moral character and parental involvement. However, we did not have sufficient data or information about the schools, and we needed them to advance our project to the next stage.

Process and Solution:

I conducted extensive online research gathering and analyzing academic articles, news articles, and other relevant information about NHA schools. Then I created a presentation that outlined my findings. One of my findings was that parents make up the moral focus committee. They bring in speakers for assemblies and activities such as family picnics and recreational events. One school established the Watch DOGS (Dads of Great Students) group to help students without fathers so they can be provided with role models in their lives. Finally, I evaluated and compared quantitative data of the grades, test scores, attendance rates, etc., of Michigan charter school students and public school students through Microsoft Excel.

As I reported my findings regarding the quantitative data, I discovered that while NHA charter schools enforce significantly more parental involvement and a larger focus on academic excellence, public schools sometimes are just as effective in educating students successfully than those charter schools.
