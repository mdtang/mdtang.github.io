APP INTRODUCTION
BRAINSTORMING

This is AgroSphere.

During my first internship, I worked at NASA Ames Research Center for the World Wind Development Team. I work was fortunately to work with an eclectic group of interns to build a web application that visualizes effects of climate change on agriculture using a large collection of climate and agriculture data and the Web World Wind Software Development Kit.

If you want to check the app out right now, here are the available links. The about page has some documentation for our development process, but I will explain my particular process in more detail below.

Research Process

During the first two weeks of the internship, we began to brainstorm for possible project ideas. Some of the ideas were:

  • Agricultural web app usable in secondary school classrooms to teach students about climate change's effects on agriculture worldwide.
  • Renewable energy web app that accurately predicts what type of renewable energy (hydroelectric, solar, wind, etc.) is optimal for a specific region.
  • Natural disasters web app that visualizes varying types of disasters around the world, showing economic and political significance, and forecasting future occurrences.

After days of intense conference room discussions and sometimes awkward silences, we decided on the agricultural web app because we believed that it would have the largest positive impact on the world, especially with the preliminary data we found. We found that the other two apps would be overly time-consuming for us to create a sufficiently-accurate algorithm during the two months we had to develop the app.

What followed was two full weeks of data scavenging, planning for possible features, and building and iterating on mockup screens.

As the designer of the team, I wanted to create a minimalistic user interface and a visual theme that fits with agriculture and climate. Being kid-friendly was also an aspect that I wanted to focus on so middle school students would not be discouraged and quit using the app.

We wanted to educate the students both quantitatively and qualitatively. Our goal was to display data using statistical analysis via graphs, as well as to include facts for specific countries with available datasets.
