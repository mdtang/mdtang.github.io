    Video Game Tracking Mobile Application

  • UX Design and Research

Background and Goal

From personal experience and frequent discussions with online friends, I realized that hardcore gamers want a dedicated application to keep track of their video game libraries so that they can monitor their progress (e.g. game-play hours) in games, compare and compete with friends between games, and manage hours spent gaming to healthy hours based on personalized recommendations.

My goal is to design a mobile application that is easily accessible and help gamers achieve these objected listed above.

Research Process

To design an app that would actually solve these real-world problems that many gamers face, I interviewed 10 gamers who I consider as "hardcore" gamers. They either own over 100 games and/or have played one game for over 500 hours. Most of the interviewees are my close connections. The interviews are semi-structured and lasted for about 30 to 40 minutes.

I asked questions in three categories:

Purchasing Habits
Playing Habits
Goals (Short-Term and Long-Term)

Personas

After these interviews, I analyzed the detailed notes I took, discovered similar patterns between my interviewees, and created two user personas based on my findings.

James, 20, College Junior

Bio

James is a third-year college student at the University of Michigan majoring in Computer Science. Even though he is very smart and diligent, James’ academic performance has been in steady decline due to the time he spends playing video games coupled with the increasing difficulty of his coursework. He is currently seeking a way to control his video game addiction while still having the freedom to enjoy playing games.

Strengths

Good at problem-solving

Determined to complete goals and often will not let up until goals are completed

Intelligent (especially book-smart)

Weaknesses

Constantly distracted by video games but struggle in managing his time efficiently

Unorganized during busy times

Expert procrastinator

Often frustrated with minute missing details

Short on money due to college debt

Personality Traits

Hard-working

Meticulous (sometimes pays too much attention to details)

Introverted; most friends are also gamers who encourage each other to play for extended sessions

Conscientious

Aloof

Goals

Seeks to get in the habit of purchasing one game a month instead of two or three a month that do not get played

Hopes to play limited number of hours per week

Favorite Game Genres

First Person Shooters
Role-Playing Games
Competitive Game

Scarlett, 22, Recent College Graduate

Bio

Scarlett is a recent graduate of the University of Michigan School of Information. Having secured a well-suited job in Ann Arbor, she now has more free time to explore interests that she did not have time to explore during her college years. Her younger brother is an avid gamer, which has peaked her curiosity in video games. Nonetheless, she is worried that she may get overwhelmed by the number of games that she has access to, despite being an organized individual. Her brother has faced this problem and she is afraid that it may occur to her as well.

Strengths

Excellent in building friendships and bonding

Organized, even during hectic times

Weaknesses

Often lazy
Procrastinator
Easily distracted

Personality Traits

Extroverted
Agreeable
Open-minded

Goals

Wants to get into video games but is looking for a more efficient way to track her own progress through each game

Favorite Game Genres

Co-operative Games
Puzzle Games
Casual Game
